# Selection Policy

Hermes should include only research that is plausibly useful for longevity enhancement and healthy aging in a 74-year-old male.

## Include preferentially

- Human RCTs, meta-analyses, systematic reviews
- Large prospective cohort studies with clinically meaningful outcomes
- Research in older adults, especially men over 65, when available
- Studies measuring function, morbidity, mortality, hospitalization, cognition, cardiovascular events, frailty, or quality of life
- Reputable sources: peer-reviewed journals, arXiv/medRxiv/bioRxiv with caution labels, NIH/CDC/WHO/NIA, major academic centers

## Deprioritize or exclude

- Marketing pages, supplement-company claims, and affiliate content
- Animal-only findings unless highly relevant and clearly labeled
- Small uncontrolled biohacker anecdotes
- Papers focused only on surrogate markers without clinical context
- Duplicate preprints or minor updates unless important
- Research with obvious conflicts of interest and weak methodology

## Scoring rubric

Score each candidate from 0 to 10:

- Relevance to 74-year-old male: 0–2
- Evidence quality: 0–3
- Clinical/practical significance: 0–2
- Novelty or field-changing value: 0–1
- Safety clarity and applicability: 0–2

Default inclusion threshold: **7.5/10**.

## Required note fields

Every included item must include:

- Citation and URL
- Date found
- Evidence type
- Population studied
- Summary
- Main finding
- Limitations
- Safety considerations
- Relevance to this repository
- Whether clinician discussion is warranted
