# Longevity Enhancement for a 74-Year-Old Male

## Scope

This dossier tracks research relevant to extending **healthspan**, preserving function, reducing disease risk, and improving resilience for a 74-year-old male.

It emphasizes practical clinical relevance over hype.

## Core objectives

- Preserve muscle mass, strength, balance, and independence
- Reduce cardiovascular, metabolic, cognitive, cancer, fracture, and infection risks
- Improve sleep, energy, cognition, and quality of life
- Identify interventions with favorable benefit/risk profiles
- Track emerging geroscience cautiously and critically

## Current priority areas

### Highest-confidence domains

- Progressive resistance training
- Aerobic conditioning and VO₂ max maintenance
- Balance/fall-prevention training
- Adequate protein and nutrition quality
- Blood pressure and lipid risk reduction
- Sleep quality and sleep apnea evaluation when indicated
- Vaccinations and infection-risk reduction
- Bone density and fracture risk management
- Medication review and deprescribing where appropriate

### Watchlist domains

- Rapamycin/rapalogs
- Metformin in non-diabetics
- GLP-1 receptor agonists and muscle preservation in older adults
- SGLT2 inhibitors for non-diabetic aging-related outcomes
- NAD precursors
- Senolytics
- Plasma/exosome/stem-cell claims
- Epigenetic clocks and biological age testing

## Review stance

For each intervention, ask:

1. Is there meaningful human evidence in older adults?
2. Does it improve hard outcomes, validated biomarkers, or functional capacity?
3. What are the risks for a 74-year-old male?
4. What interactions or contraindications matter?
5. Is this ready for clinician discussion, or only worth monitoring?
